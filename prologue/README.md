# Prologue — Before You Begin

> **You are here.** This is the orientation every Razorpayan goes through before picking a belt. Read it in order. It's the shortest part of the whole playbook, and it's the part that makes the rest make sense.

---

## What the Prologue gives you

By the time you finish the 12 chapters below you will:

1. Know **why** this playbook exists and **who** it's for — including you.
2. Understand the **origin story** (why our first attempt failed and what changed on the second) and why every White Belt chapter ends with "what colour are you?"
3. Have a working **mental model** of the 5-layer AI dev stack — the picture you can draw on a whiteboard.
4. Know the **9-layer Enablement Stack** — the map of what you're climbing across the belts.
5. Be able to tell Claude Code apart from Slash, Cowork, Compass, and Claude.ai in one sentence each.
6. Know **which roles and forums** can unblock you, and **where to ask** (hint: start in [`#claude-onboarding-support`](https://razorpay.slack.com/archives/C0ANCMTCJA2)).
7. Know how to **read this playbook as a reader**, and (if you lead a team) how to **use it as a manager**.
8. Know **which belt to start at**.
9. Know **what never goes into a prompt** (the safety brief).
10. See **what's shipping this week**, because this is a living program, not a static manual.

## How to read the Prologue

- **If you have 10 minutes right now:** read 0.1, 0.2, and skim 0.5.
- **If you have a morning:** read all 12 in order.
- **If you're a manager planning adoption for your POD:** read 0.1, 0.2, 0.4, and 0.9.
- **If you've already attended a Builder Day:** 0.1 and 0.10 (self-assessment) are the priority. You probably belong at Yellow Belt.

You don't earn anything for finishing the Prologue — there's no quest, no boss fight. The reward is that the rest of the playbook stops feeling like a pile of new vocabulary and starts feeling like a map.
