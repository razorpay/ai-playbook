# Boss Fight 0B — Automate one workflow that saves 4+ hours/week

> **🏁 Boss fight · ⏱ 2 weeks · 🎯 Win condition:** one real workflow automated end-to-end, measured for two weeks, and documented so a teammate can fork it.

---

## The premise

Ops 101 is not complete because you read eight chapters. It is complete when your week is materially lighter.

The boss fight asks you to pick the highest-leverage entry from your agent diary, configure the workflow, run it for two weeks, and show the evidence. The target is simple: **4+ hours saved per week**.

If that sounds high, good. A boss fight should not be a worksheet. It should change your calendar.

---

## Pick the workflow

Use the diary from Quest 0B-2. The right workflow usually has five traits:

- it happens at least weekly;
- it takes 30+ minutes each time, or creates delay for multiple people;
- the inputs are accessible through an approved surface or connector;
- the output has a repeatable shape;
- a human can review the output before it affects anyone else.

Do not pick a workflow whose first version requires sensitive data transmission, money movement, irreversible changes, or judgement you cannot explain.

---

## Build the automation

Your automation can be a configured Claude project, a Cowork workflow, a Slash pattern, a connector-backed recipe, a lightweight agent, or a documented prompt-and-review process. The surface matters less than the repeatability.

It must have:

- a named trigger;
- named inputs;
- a clear output format;
- a review step;
- a failure path;
- a place where the recipe is documented.

The review step is required. The first version should make you faster, not invisible.

---

## Run it for two weeks

For each run, capture:

- date;
- time spent before automation would have run;
- time spent with automation;
- manual review time;
- quality issues found;
- whether you trusted the output enough to use it.

Two weeks is long enough to expose whether the workflow actually holds up. One impressive demo does not count.

---

## Required evidence

Submit a short boss-fight note with:

- the workflow name;
- the old process;
- the new process;
- the AI surface or agent used;
- two weeks of timing evidence;
- before/after examples with sensitive details removed;
- the review checklist;
- the documented recipe for a teammate;
- the weekly hours saved.

If you saved less than four hours, document the result anyway. You may still have built a useful recipe, but the boss fight is not complete until the impact clears the bar or you pick a better workflow.

---

## Success criteria

You pass Boss Fight 0B when:

- the automation ran for two weeks;
- it saved 4+ hours per week or removed equivalent team delay;
- the output was reviewed by a human before use;
- the recipe is safe under the Prologue safety brief;
- a teammate could fork the recipe without a private walkthrough.

---

## What you can say after Boss Fight 0B

> "I can identify routine ops work, turn it into a safe AI-assisted workflow, measure the time saved, and leave behind a recipe my team can reuse."

That is the Ops 101 outcome. Not curiosity. Not prompt fluency. A lighter week, proven.
